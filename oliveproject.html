Project Overview

The Story

As a Carrerfoundry student in the UX design immersion course, I had the task of designing a responsive web app.

I decided to use the OLIVE Health app. In addition to allowing users to set goals and new habits to improve their lifestyle, the app also needed to allow users to make live video calls with coaches or doctors to improve their health.

Understanding the problem

At the beginning of my design process, I define the core problem I want to solve.

Problem definition

People who are under a lot of stress in their professional or personal lives need a way to adopt a better lifestyle with new goals and habits.

We will know this is true when these people report that they are more relaxed in their daily lives and feel better psychologically.

User Research

Competitive analysis

Next, I want to find out what similar services and products are already on the market and how they solve the problem I want to solve. To this end, I analyzed and reviewed two potential competitors for my product, the Mindshine app and Remente app.

What I liked most about the Mindshine app was that users were able to formulate their own goals in addition to set goals. This way, people can adopt new habits at their own pace without overwhelming themselves at the beginning.

Based on this research, I created user stories to drive my app project.

User Stories

  • As a user, I want to be able to search and find other features of the app in an easy to understand menu when I need them to give me new ideas for new goals.
  • As a user who uses the app daily, I would like to have a calendar feature that allows me to track my last few days to give me an overview of my performance so far.
  • As a user, I would like to receive daily tips and advice from the app to learn more about health and develop new habits on a daily basis.
  • When I create an account, I want to have an onboarding process to get to know the app better so I can use it more effectively.
  • When I load a new health app on my phone, I want to get through the sign-up process quickly so I can get help and advice as soon as possible.

User Interviews

To further explore the problem area, I conducted 4 Skype interviews with users from the target group and used affinity mapping to organize the data and capture the results.

Research objectives

  1. To determine the wants and needs that led them to download a health or fitness app.
  2. to understand how users feel when they succeed in integrating new habits into their daily lives and achieving their set goals
  3. deeper understanding of how users learn about health and fitness, and what motivates them and plays a role in doing so.

Key insights

All respondents downloaded health or fitness apps for different reasons. Motives include losing weight, staying physically active or finding out about an acute illness. Many of the users also said they were not motivated enough to use an app regularly over a longer period of time.

The key to arousing their interest in a health app is through an individualized questionnaire. This ensures that the app responds to the user's wishes and needs and suggests suitable goals and advice. This can be achieved through a personalized questionnaire. In order to constantly maintain motivation, it also makes sense to integrate a reward system.

Concept

User Personas

The use of qualitative and quantitative research methods proved to be an excellent means of identifying the characteristic needs and goals of the user group I was targeting.

These personas formed the basis for my information hierarchy and the general flow of the app, which was tailored to their goals and needs. This helped me empathize with my target audience and create two user personas: Mark and Jenny.

Task analysis and user flow

From the perspective of my user personas, I created the user journeys, task analysis, and user flows for the key features of my app.

User Journeys

I had created user journey maps for my user personas Mark and Jenny. This way, I could put myself in their shoes and get a better idea of how they want to achieve their goals.

User Flow

User flow was especially important in determining the scene needed and the general concept of a site map

Task Analysis

  • Task: search for goals against burnout in the goal setting menu and manage notifications in the profile.
  • Entry point: open the web app
  • Success Criteria: Goal(s) accepted and notifications enabled.

Ideation

Wireframes and Prototype

After interviewing users and gathering all the information needed to understand the user and their needs, I created a paper prototype at the beginning of the design phase. This helped me sort out my thoughts and get an overall idea of what forms my design could take. As I gained a deeper understanding of the concept, I created medium quality wireframes that I then merged into an initial prototype in Adobe XD/Figma.

Usability Testing

The only way to verify the usefulness of a design or prototype is to test it with real people. For this reason, I conducted a preference test on Usability Hub. This was to find out which design of physician information would resonate better with participants. There were 11 participants in total. Also, basic functions were to be tested:

  1. Test how easy it is for the new user to log in. Are there any friction points or hesitation in disclosing information?
  2. Can the user follow the onboarding information and set and view their first new destination?
  3. How easy is it to book a call with a doctor? Are all the options given that the user wants?
  4. Does the user understand how to keep the booked appointment with the doctor?

Conclusion

Trust in the process

Like many designers, I'm always tempted to jump right into the visual design and start too early with the UI details. Applying design thinking to his project reminded me once again that not every phase of a project needs to be perfect before moving on.

Incremental improvements based on feedback from real users are at the heart of the iterative process. Design is never finished, but working on the Olive project showed me that trusting the process is worthwhile and also helped me internalize that done is better than perfect.
